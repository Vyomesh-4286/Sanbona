Uncover the story Behind sanbona

Sanbona finds its roots lodged deeply within South Africa's rich history. You will discover San rock paintings dating back more than 3,500 years amongst the vast array of indigenous fauna and flora. The landscapes and wildlife beg you to admire them up-close, with twice-daily game drives, nature walks, birdwatching, photography, rock art tours, and stargazing activities. After a day of exploring, unwind with our various relaxation retreat offerings. Little ones will also be entertained at our Gondwana Family Lodge, with plenty of child-friendly facilities and our Kids on Safari activities.

Accommodation

A Mindful Safari in the Middle of Nowhere

Sanbona Wildlife Reserve invites you for a high-end, conservation-based nature safari in the vast and varied landscapes of the unique Little Karoo. It's the closest true wildlife experience you can find near Cape Town, a short three-hour drive away, offering exclusive and unique malaria-free safaris. From the classic Tilney Manor to Gondwana Family Lodge welcoming children of all ages, there's something for everyone. Couples will love Dwyka Tented Lodge, set in a secluded horseshoe bend, and if you’re looking for something a bit more adventurous, Sanbona Explorer Camp offers an immersive walking safari experience.

Space and Solitude

Our extraordinary, vast landscapes truly set us apart from other safari options. It keeps you guessing, showing off something new around every turn. You'll marvel at the little things, morning fog clinging to a fascinating cactus, your guide's stories weaving together a bigger picture and understanding of the world around you. At the foot of the Warmwaterberg Mountains, Sanbona stretches across 58,000ha of wide-open plains, with mountain tops to gorgeous gorges and a wide array of untamed flowers and foliage. Enter our ethereal safari destination, where you'll become one with your surroundings, reaching the ultimate level of inner peace.

Conservation

Conserving Ancient heritage, ecosystems and landscapes

Sanbona's unique setting and delicate ecosystem is something we respect and strive to protect with everything we do. Conservation is a core value at Sanbona, and, with every visit, you play a vital part in preserving the region for future generations. What started as a dream in 2011 is a reality today. We're proud to be a highly respected player in conservation as we continue to guard the area's heritage, ecosystem and landscapes while creating employment opportunities to uplift the local communities.
